What is a cookie?

Cookies are baked sweets made mostly from flour, sugar, and fat (usually butter). They come in a huge range of textures, from soft and chewy to crispy and crunchy, depending on ingredients and baking time.

The story of cookies

Cookies started in 7th-century Persia, where sugar was first widely used. As trade grew, the idea spread to Europe by the Middle Ages, where cookies became popular because they stayed fresh and were easy to carry on long trips.
